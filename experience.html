This photo represents a crucial milestone in my tenure as President of the Campus Election Chair during Semester 5 of my diploma studies.
Leading the election process at UiTM Kelantan was both difficult and gratifying, necessitating careful organisation, collaboration, and strong leadership.
As a critical part of the leadership team, I was responsible for ensuring that the election process was fair, transparent, and efficient.
We achieved an amazing voter turnout thanks to our combined efforts, with UiTM Kelantan achieving an incredible 96% participation rate—a significant increase from 91% the previous session.
This success represents not just our students' devotion, but also our team's dedication to establishing a culture of active student involvement and democratic participation.
This experience dramatically changed my leadership, cooperation, and organisational abilities,
enabling me to handle problems, encourage collaboration, and make important contributions to student governance.
It was an honour to collaborate with such a passionate and committed team, all working towards a common goal of amplifying student voices and building our campus community.


The program, "international volunteering program series 2: Let's stand together with prime mover UITM Kelantan branch x Universitas Utara Sumatra Medan Indonesia,"
required nine prime mover club delegates to travel to Indonesia while still serving as our President of the Campus Election Chair.
Five external collaborations were also involved. This is my second overseas program; it is an easy thing because I have three programs coming up soon.
We are exposed to the culture, customs, and traditional cuisine of Indonesia through our concentration
on digital marketing in the mangrove love coal and your Dali Serdang market.
We also got to know Chinese and Thai students who participated in the program, in addition to Indonesians.


In order to give us a firsthand look at the village's culture, we were split up into 11 host families, each of whom had two participants.
Making traditional "wau" kites, touring a serunding factory to discover how it is made, and engaging in net fishing were some of our activities.
In addition, we participated in a CSR project to clean the village mosque, presented a math program at Sekolah Rendah Integrasi Teras Islam Al-Furqan (SRITI) school,
and had fun engaging with the community in the evenings through a wayang kulit performance.
Through these activities, we developed deep relationships with the residents and deepened our awareness of the village's customs.

With an emphasis on volunteerism and community service, the Ministry of Higher Education (KPT) founded the "Skuad Ihsan Madani" to promote compassion and solidarity.
Many public universities (IPTAs), including UniSZA, UMT, UPNM, UPM, UTEM, UTM, UPSI, UniMAP, and many more,
are involved in the flood relief mission in Kelantan, which is one of its primary initiatives.
Along with giving afflicted areas much-needed assistance, these initiatives improve cooperation and create enduring friendships and togetherness among students from different universities.
